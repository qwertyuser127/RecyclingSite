The Recycling Problem

Students often mix waste or use the wrong bin. This leads to contamination, meaning recyclable materials get thrown away instead of reused.

Why it matters

Up to 30% of school recycling is lost due to contamination.

Top 15 Recycling Problems (Priority Ranked)

1. High Contamination Rates

Food residue ruins entire recycling batches.

2. Incorrect Bin Sorting

Students frequently choose the wrong bin.

3. Lack of Recycling Education

Most students don't know sorting rules.

4. Confusing Bin Labels

Icons and colors are unclear.

5. Single-Use Plastics

Packaging dominates school waste.

6. Overflowing Recycling Bins

Bins fill up too fast.

7. E-Waste Mishandling

Chargers and batteries thrown in general waste.

8. No Digital Tools

No smart assistant or scanner.

9. Dirty Recyclables

Students don't rinse containers.

10. Low Motivation

Students don't see recycling impact.

11. Inconsistent Rules

Different areas follow different rules.

12. Hidden Recyclables

Foil and cartons confuse people.

13. Poor Bin Placement

Bins placed in bad locations.

14. No Feedback

No stats or progress shown.

15. Misleading Packaging Labels

"Recyclable" logos confuse people.

Impact of Smart Recycling

Our goal: cut contamination by 50% and double correct bin usage in schools.
